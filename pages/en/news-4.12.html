Came from the Silk Road of the crops

2017-08-04 Source: China Science News 46

Recently, the online spread of an article entitled "Qin Dynasty in the end what to eat?" Post. Let people sigh, the original Qin Dynasty food choice is very limited, the cooking method is still in its infancy. Many of the crops we eat are imported from abroad in the long historical period.

Dr. Liu Qizhen of Nanjing Agricultural University, Chinese Academy of agricultural civilization, the "science news" Chinese told reporters, according to statistics, in Chinese existing crops (mainly refers to crops, vegetables and fruit), at least 50 species from abroad. Most of them were introduced into China through land and sea silk routes.

According to Liu Qizhen introduction, the silk road is an important link of ancient Chinese civilization and cultural connections to the outside world, a large number of overseas crops with the development of the silk road was introduced, not only increased the crop type, and agricultural production and the diet structure changes in the China had a very important influence. In our country's existing crops, the name has "West", "Hu", "fan", basically come from outside the country.

In 1492, Columbo discovered America's new continent. In late sixteenth Century, the Spanish colonies in Philippines, some American crops began to spread to Philippines, and from Philippines to Southeast Asia, and further spread to China, then, is the Ming and Qing Dynasties in china. The introduction and dissemination of American crops became a notable feature of the introduction of crops in the Ming and Qing dynasties. According to records, the introduction of crops are corn, sweet potato, potato, cassava, peanuts, sunflower, pepper, tomato, pineapple, cashew, cocoa, tobacco and other nearly 30.

The crops introduced into China in the Han, Tang and Ming dynasties have very distinctive different features. Simply speaking, the Han and Tang Dynasties spread to our country crops to fruits and vegetables, the staple food is mainly introduced after the Ming dynasty. The function of the two is different." Liu Qizhen explained that the food crops introduced into our country after the Ming Dynasty were "help" crops. "Sweet potato, corn, potatoes are basically for life-saving crops, from the Ming Dynasty after the Chinese population exceeded sixty million, one hundred million, two hundred million, to the late Qing Dynasty has exceeded four hundred million." Many exotic crops have been introduced and cultivated in china. Most of these plants have been passed down for a long time, becoming relatively common agricultural crops, while others are just a flash in the pan and become a passing passer-by. This mechanism of retention or elimination has both natural and social factors. "First of all, it has something to do with our soil, climate and other natural environment. Secondly, it is related to the tastes of the Chinese people.". Sometimes, the food that foreign people like to eat doesn't always conform to the taste of the Chinese, and its spread and promotion in China will be limited." Liu Qizhen said.

During the Han and Tang Dynasties, most of the crops introduced in China originated in Western Asia, and some originated from the Mediterranean coast, Africa or India. Most of them were imported by the "Silk Road" on the land.
