In 1926, presided over by the archaeologist Li Ji, in Yuncheng City, Xia County Wei Guo Xiang Xi Yin Cun heights, discovered a Neolithic cultural sites, a semi artificial cocoon "specimen" cutting down the unearthed cultural relics, through research and analysis, is believed to be the ancestors of the silkworm. As Mr. Li Ji said in the "Shanxi Xi Yin Cun of prehistoric sites" in an article: "our most interesting finding is half a cocoon 1.5 cutting silk like, with a microscope study, half of the broken in half, but still light, the cutting part of the flat.Because it was separated by workers."

In 1928, the Smith Institute in Washington, D. C., identified the half cocoon and confirmed: "in the ruins of the painted pottery culture in West Yin village, half of the individual workers cut out the cocoons." It's the ancestor of the silkworm. Silk culture is something invented and developed in China, which is an eternal fact. After that, Japanese scholars and Ume Shunro also carried out the rehabilitation appraisal respectively. In a word, the opinions of scholars and experts are highly consistent.

It is said that this half silkworm cocoon was originally placed in the Central Museum and later collected and preserved in the the Imperial Palace Museum in Taiwan.
