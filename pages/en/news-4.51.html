ASEAN Students: "The Belt and Road" make our future more broad

2017-07-27 Source: Guangming Daily 35

With more and more exchanges between China and ASEAN countries, more and more ASEAN students choose to study in china. The latest statistics from the Chinese Ministry of education show that the number of ASEAN Students from China increased from about 50 thousand in 2010 to more than 80 thousand in 2016. Among them, the number of students studying abroad in Guizhou became geometrically multiple growth. In 2016, the number of Guizhou students enrolled in ASEAN countries increased by 509 times compared with 2008, reaching 1528 people.

The ASEAN China students choose to study, in realizing their ideal of life at the same time, they will also become a bridge for China Unicom The Belt and Road along the country, become "The Belt and Road" people connected to power.

Vietnamese student, Hainan is one of them. By the end of 2013, after many layers of selection and fierce competition, Hainan received the acceptance letter and first class scholarship from the Guizhou national university. In addition to tuition and accommodation fees, there are still 1000 yuan per month for living expenses, which allows Hainan to study at ease here, and fully aware of what has happened here.

Hainan said: "my initial choice of human resources management is the sign of the people's major, in addition to learning advanced professional concepts, language ability will become my future career important weight."

"Learning in the past few years Chinese, I saw a lot of new technology is not our home, like mobile phone payment, online shopping, greatly facilitate people's lives, I am still a" purchasing ", I help Vietnamese customers to buy sports shoes, from Chinese electronic products. They've got a good bargain, and I've found business opportunities." Hainan said, with frequent bilateral trade, especially in the background of "The Belt and Road", Vietnam's agricultural products can be more easily transported to China, clothing, household appliances and transportation China to Vietnam will be more convenient.

"In the future, I'll probably open a Tmall Vietnamese pavilion that sells Vietnamese agricultural products and sells Chinese cheap and fair products to Vietnam via the internet." Hainan said that after more than 3 years of learning, he broadened his horizons, exposed many things that he had never heard before, and made a better sense of China's development.
