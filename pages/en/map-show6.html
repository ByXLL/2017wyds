Li Jiahong introduced, in the global large-scale grain and oil crop production situation research, remote sensing monitoring analysis found that in 2015 the global bulk grain and oil crops production amounted to 27.65 tons, an increase of 4 million 760 thousand tons, an increase of 0.2%. Among them, the global wheat production was 724 million 320 thousand tons, an increase of 2 million 490 thousand tons, an increase of 0.3%; global corn production was 990 million 320 thousand tons, with basically the same as in 2014; the global rice production was 742 million tons, compared with 2014 production of 1 million 110 thousand tons, a decrease of 0.1%; global soybean production was 308 million 790 thousand tons, an increase of 2 million 960 thousand tons, an increase of 1%. Affected by strong El Nino, Indonesia rice, South African corn, Ethiopia corn and wheat production decreased significantly, but the global bulk crop production is still unchanged from 2014.

In 2015, China's total grain output was 568 million 80 thousand tons, an increase of 4 million 310 thousand tons compared with 2014, an increase of 0.8%. Among them, the total grain output was 125 million 700 thousand tons, compared to 2014 increased by 2 million 160 thousand tons, an increase of 1.7%; the total grain output was 407 million 260 thousand tons, an increase of 2 million 420 thousand tons, an increase of 0.6%. Strong El Ni o, as well as pests and diseases, did not significantly affect the yield of China's major grain and oil crops.

In 2016, Australia, Brazil and Argentina three southern hemisphere wheat producing countries is expected to total yield of wheat was 42 million 966 thousand tons, compared to the year 2015 will be reduced to 1 million 378 thousand tons; by severe drought, South Africa corn average yield fell 16%, corn acreage fell sharply by 34%, is expected in 2016 South Africa total corn production will drop to 7 million 321 thousand tons, compared to the same period production of 5 million 886 thousand tons, a decline of up to 44.6%; China, summer grain output by lower yields and adjusting the dual effects of planting structure, is expected to decline to 121 million 770 thousand tons, a decline of 2.9%, the total yield of winter wheat is expected to be 111 million 210 thousand tons, representing an annual reduction of 2%.
