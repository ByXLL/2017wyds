Japanese media: African countries expect China's railway construction to stimulate economic vitality

2017-08-08 Source: World Wide Web 35

China is speeding up rail construction in Africa, according to Japan's economic news reported on August 8th. Opened in Kenya, capital of Nairobi and the connection port of Mombasa Mongolian railway, will be completed in the construction of Ethiopia to neighbouring Djibouti port railway. All parties are looking forward to stimulating logistics and economic vitality.

"This is the beginning of a new history of Kenya," the president of Kenya, Kenyatta, stressed to the overseas press when the high-speed rail link between Kenya capital Nairobi and trade port Mombasa opened in May. The opening of the railway helped drive exports to industries that are increasingly gathering in the capital Nairobi. In addition, the railway is planned to extend to Rwanda, Uganda, Burundi and Ethiopia.

In neighbouring Kenya, Ethiopia, the railways linking the capital Addisababa and Djibouti will also be opened soon. Ethiopia is a landlocked country, and the construction of a railway that can transport large quantities of goods to export ports has been a subject of constant concern. It would take 3 days to use a truck, and if it were to use the railway, it could be shortened to less than 12 hours.

In West Africa, the railways connecting Nigeria, the capital, Abuja and Kaduna opened in 2016. Plans are also being extended to Lagos, a commercial city in the coastal region. The construction of these railway networks is aided by china. Construction of infrastructure in Africa is one of the project "The Belt and Road" initiative.

In May at the "The Belt and Road" International Cooperation Forum, Ethiopia and Kenya with the leaders of European and Asian countries leaders appeared together. "China is a reliable partner to help us out of poverty and achieve prosperity," said Ethiopia Prime Minister Haier, who spoke on the stage".

China's financial aid scale is very large, the report said. According to the Johns Hopkins University survey, the transport infrastructure in Africa is the largest in china. Between 2000 and 2015, China imposed about $30 billion in financing for africa. In terms of total investment in railway projects, Kenya is estimated at about $3 billion, with Ethiopia at about $4 billion, most of which are financing in china.

Not only is China concerned about Africa's rail demand. In Mozambique, Mitsui and Brazil resources, railways and ports operated by vale, opened in May. Mozambique President Niuxi expectantly said, "the completion of the railway and port contribute to industrial development".

The West African country of Benin and Niger between the two countries government is promoting the construction of the railway and the French boluolai comprehensive enterprise group. It will build about 1000 km of rail linking Niger and Benin ports, and plans to link Ivory Coast in the future. This line is expected as the West African rail link.
