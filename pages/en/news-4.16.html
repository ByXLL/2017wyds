Use a brush to get across the Silk Road

2017-07-30 Source: Xi'an daily 46

2000 years ago, the businessmen of ancient thinkers in the camel's company, starting from Changan, across the Eurasian continent, sand, snow, out of a legendary and romantic world's silk road. 2000 years later, a painter, picked up a brush back up his easel. Walking on the road of legend, with thousands of paintings to the world tell the past and present of the silk road. Recently, the reporter interviewed the painter on the Silk Road - Wang Shangxin.

Wang Shangxin loved painting since childhood. He studied oil paintings with Cai Liang and Zhang Yiqian, a famous oil painter, and he was good at portraits. Although it has been over sixty years of age, but he still need to spend a lot of time daily training painting. He said, he is not the art of painting "Coban", when he was young because of the love of painting came to Xi'an Academy of Fine Arts, happened to see the television broadcast of Japanese scholars on the silk road research monograph, popular shock. Since the Silk Road originated in China and was opened by the Chinese, I, as a Chinese, should contribute to the study of the silk road!" Thus, 30 years, Wang Shangxin along the Western Han Dynasty Zhang Qian opened the Silk Road painting, he is not sketching, that is, on the road to sketching. Accumulated thick piles of manuscripts, paintings, "on the road" of the story is to let him unforgettable. From Changan to Rome, this is Han Zhang Qian opened up the Silk Road, along the way these countries and places of Wang Hisanobu it had learned by heart.

Wang Shangxin in the intense work, always remember their dreams slightly spare on the Silk Road, sketching out. For decades, through the Shaanxi, Gansu and Ningxia, Qinghai, new provinces, along the Silk Road along the sketch, draw over the Hexi Corridor Oasis, the north and south of the Tianshan and Pamir Plateau snowy breeze. Only Mogao Grottoes in Dunhuang has gone 4 times. Reporters saw in Wang Shangxin's books, painting, murals in the caves of each record size was almost every murals, each cave has he stopped the clinical learning figure.

In addition to walking home, Wang Shangxin also held an individual painting exhibition in Dortmund, Germany. The exhibition was called "China Silk Road style painting exhibition"". In one month extension, Wang Shangxin shows the Changan goose dawn, Ji Huashan to the German people in Northern Shaanxi, Ansai, Mizhi Poyi drum, the western Kuche Kashi stone forest, especially the "Moonlight; Shaanxi ten strange" series, vividly drawn Shaanxi folk customs, the highly acclaimed. More than 50 paintings, aroused a lot of audience on the Silk Road Chinese yearn for the local media wrote in the report: "watch the magnificent gorgeous Yili horse, taste the unique flavor of Nang, then enjoy the exciting shaanxi. If his paintings can arouse people's desire to travel, they can arouse people's longing for Xi'an culture in their hometown."

Among the many creations of the Silk Road, one group is particularly attractive. The painting combines watercolors and paintings, oil paintings and sketches of the halo elements, this group is the creation of his poor tour of europe. "In 2011, I returned to Europe with her daughter, a folk painting, at their own expense to Italy, Holland, France, Belgium and other countries." Wang Shangxin said he painted sketches and wrote travel notes every time he arrived. The scroll pictures recorded the rain in Florence, the water city, the night markets in Venice, Copenhagen, and the windmill town in Holland. Poor although hard, but he was in his own happiness within: "Silk Road" in Europe, "added too important!"

Wang Shangxin's Silk Road paintings are still missing the part of central asia. Now his wish is to visit Central Asia and paint the local conditions and customs. "Man's life should have a theme, and my theme is to draw the silk road.". The scroll painting has the beginning and the end. In the future, it is necessary to draw Central Asia and open up its own silk road!" Wang Shangxin says.
